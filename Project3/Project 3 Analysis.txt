Overfitting Analysis
* Does the model overfit? No, the model does not show significant overfitting,
 as my loss comparison graph shows both the train loss and val loss trend downward together. 
 Overfitting was reduced because of the three types of data augmentation 
 (rotation, zoom, and color jitter) and Dropout layer.

Image Misclassification
* Example 1 (Compact vs. Sedan): The model confused a compact and a sedan, due to the scale of 
the sedan being challenging to establish without any comparable objects.
* Example 2 (Convertible vs. Coupe): The model confused a coupe and convertible, due to the
 convertible having a retractable hardtop that is virtually indistinguishable from a coupe.

Classification Deep-Dive
i. ImageNet Similarity and Feature Fit
    * Difference from ImageNet: The dataset is similar to ImageNet, which also contains thousands of car-related images.
    * Feature Fit: The pre-trained features were a great fit for the data, as MobileNetV2 
    was already trained to recognize wheels, windows, and metallic surfaces. 
ii. Flatten vs. Global Average Pooling (GAP)
    * Trainable Parameters: Switching from a GAP layer to a Flatten layer would drastically 
    increase the number of trainable parameters, as using a Flatten layer would significantly
    increase the risk of overfitting. The massive increase in parameters would allow the model 
    to "memorize" the training data more easily, leading to a large performance gap between training and validation.
